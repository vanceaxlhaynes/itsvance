I go weeks without noise. This is a small corner of the internet where words are left to breathe. Poetry, prose, and the occasional thought written in the hours between the noise. There's beauty in keeping things. And always faith in the quiet. Thank you for finding this place. -a quiet place

Woke up before the alarm. The rain had that particular sound — the kind that makes you feel like staying is the bravest thing you can do. So I stayed. Made tea instead of coffee because it felt like a tea kind of morning. Sometimes the day tells you what it wants before you get a chance to decide. Read for an hour. Didn't finish the chapter. That's fine. The book will wait. Books are patient like that — infinitely more patient than people. Called home in the afternoon. Nobody answered. Left a voicemail that was mostly silence and then "I was just thinking about you." Hung up feeling like I'd said exactly enough. The evening came softly. Cooked something simple. Ate slowly. Watched the light leave the room without turning on a lamp.
-a quiet place

There's a bookshop on the corner of a street I can't remember the name of. It doesn't matter — the books remember me. That's the strange thing about places you love: they hold onto you even when you forget to hold onto them. The owner is an older man who wears the same cardigan regardless of the season. He doesn't greet you when you walk in. He just nods, the way someone acknowledges a returning bird. You were here. You left. You came back. That's the whole conversation. I go there when the world feels too loud. Not to read, exactly, but to be near reading. Near the possibility of someone else's words solving something I didn't know was broken. Last Tuesday I found a first edition of something I'd been looking for without knowing I was looking for it. That's how the best things find you. Not through searching. Through staying open long enough to be found.
-a quiet place

Nothing extraordinary happened today and that itself felt extraordinary. Made breakfast without checking my phone first. Eggs, toast, the last of the jam from that jar I bought at the market three weeks ago. Walked to the park. Sat on the bench that faces the wrong direction — the one that looks at the wall instead of the pond. I like that bench. It feels like it chose to be different, and nobody moved it. A stranger nodded at me. I nodded back. We shared something small and unnamed. That's community, isn't it? Not the grand gestures. Just the small acknowledgments that say: I see you. You're here. That's enough. Came home. Opened the window even though it was cold. Let the sound of the street in. Wrote these words. Felt like a good day. I think it was.
-a quiet place

I've been walking slower lately. Not because my legs are tired, but because I've been noticing things. A crack in the pavement that looks like a river delta. The way a shadow from a tree moves like a slow hand across the ground. Speed is a thief. I don't mean that in the motivational-poster way. I mean it literally steals moments from you — moments you paid for by being alive. I passed a window today and there was a woman inside arranging flowers. She didn't know I was watching. She held each stem like it was a small decision, turning it slowly before placing it down. She was building something beautiful, and she was doing it for no one. That's the thing about walking slowly. You start to see the people who are living on purpose. The ones who aren't rushing toward anything. They're just here — fully, quietly, completely here.
-a quiet place
